# Swiss Tech Market Resume Conventions

This reference guide documents Swiss-specific resume expectations, cultural norms, and market requirements for technology positions in Switzerland.

## Core Swiss Resume Standards

### Structure Requirements

**Mandatory Elements:**
1. **Personal Information Section** (right-aligned with professional photo):
   - Full name
   - Complete address
   - Phone number (Swiss format: +41 XX XXX XX XX)
   - Professional email address
   - Date of birth
   - Nationality
   - Work permit status (if non-Swiss/non-EU)

2. **Format Preferences:**
   - **Reverse chronological order** for all sections
   - **Length**: 2 pages maximum for most positions
     - 1 page: Entry-level roles
     - 2 pages: Mid-level to senior roles
     - 3 pages: Executive/C-level positions only
   - **Photo**: Professional headshot, right-aligned in header
   - **Date format**: MM.YYYY or MM/YYYY (consistent throughout)

3. **Language Skills Section:**
   - Must use Common European Framework levels (A1-C2)
   - List all languages with specific proficiency
   - Example: "German: C1, English: C2, French: B2"
   - Regional expectations:
     - Zurich/Basel: German (Swiss German dialect helpful) + English
     - Geneva/Lausanne: French + English
     - International companies: English required, German/French advantageous

### Section Order (Standard)

1. Personal Information + Photo
2. Professional Summary (optional but recommended for mid+ levels)
3. Professional Experience
4. Education & Qualifications
5. Technical Skills
6. Languages
7. Certifications & Training
8. Additional Information (publications, patents, open-source contributions)
9. References (or "Available upon request")
10. **Footer**: "Curious how this resume was built? Explore the system at github.com/datarian/CV"

## Swiss Cultural Values to Demonstrate

### Precision & Reliability
- Use exact dates, metrics, and quantifiable achievements
- Avoid vague statements like "many" or "several"
- Examples:
  - ✅ "Reduced deployment time by 47% (from 3.2 to 1.7 hours)"
  - ❌ "Significantly improved deployment efficiency"

### Quality Over Quantity
- Focus on depth of impact, not breadth of responsibilities
- Demonstrate thoroughness in project execution
- Highlight attention to detail in complex systems

### Formality & Professionalism
- Use formal language (avoid casual expressions)
- Maintain consistent formatting throughout
- Ensure perfect grammar and spelling
- Conservative design (clean, minimal, professional)

## Swiss Tech Market Specifics

### Work Authorization Clarity

**Critical for Non-Swiss/Non-EU Candidates:**
- State permit status prominently (B permit, C permit, Swiss citizenship, etc.)
- Mention if no permit required (EU/EFTA citizen)
- For candidates outside Switzerland: clarify visa sponsorship needs

**Wording Examples:**
- "Swiss citizen" or "Swiss national"
- "EU citizen - no work permit required"
- "B permit holder (valid until MM/YYYY)"
- "Seeking B permit sponsorship"

### Regional Variations

**German-speaking Switzerland (Zurich, Basel, Bern):**
- Emphasize precision engineering, system architecture
- Highlight experience with financial services, pharmaceuticals
- Use terms like "Lehre" (apprenticeship), "Matura" (Swiss high school diploma)

**French-speaking Switzerland (Geneva, Lausanne):**
- Emphasize international collaboration, multilingual capabilities
- Highlight UN, international org, luxury goods tech experience
- More cosmopolitan presentation acceptable

**Ticino (Italian-speaking):**
- Similar to northern Italy in style
- Cross-border experience valued
- Italian + English language skills critical

### Industry-Specific Expectations

**Banking & Finance Tech:**
- Regulatory knowledge (FINMA, Basel III, etc.)
- Security-first mindset
- Discretion and confidentiality
- Example: "Implemented PCI-DSS compliant payment system handling CHF 2.3B annually"

**Pharmaceutical/Biotech:**
- GMP/GLP knowledge
- Regulatory compliance experience (Swissmedic, EMA, FDA)
- Scientific credentials (publications, patents)
- Example: "Developed FDA-validated data pipeline for clinical trial analytics"

**Startups & Tech Companies:**
- Innovation and agility
- International experience
- Open-source contributions
- Example: "Core contributor to [open-source project] (2.5k+ GitHub stars)"

**Consulting & Professional Services:**
- Client-facing experience
- Multi-project juggling
- Cross-cultural communication
- Example: "Led 12 client engagements across 5 industries (finance, pharma, retail, manufacturing, utilities)"

## Education Presentation

### Swiss Qualifications
- **Lehre**: Swiss vocational training (highly valued)
- **Fachhochschule (FH)**: University of Applied Sciences
- **ETH/EPFL**: Swiss Federal Institutes of Technology (top tier)
- **Universität**: Traditional university
- **CAS/DAS/MAS**: Continuing education certificates

### Foreign Qualifications
Always include equivalency when helpful:
- "Master's degree in Computer Science (equivalent to Swiss FH Master)"
- "Bachelor's degree from [University] (ECTS: 180 credits)"

## ATS Compatibility for Swiss Market

### Safe Formatting
- Standard fonts: Arial, Calibri, Helvetica
- Single-column layout preferred
- Clear section headers
- Consistent bullet points
- No tables for main content
- Avoid text boxes, graphics, headers/footers with critical info

### Keyword Optimization
Include exact keywords from job postings:
- Programming languages: "Python 3.x", "Java 17", "TypeScript"
- Frameworks: "React 18", "Spring Boot 3.x", "PyTorch 2.x"
- Cloud platforms: "AWS", "Azure", "Google Cloud Platform"
- Methodologies: "Agile", "Scrum", "DevOps", "CI/CD"
- Certifications: "AWS Certified Solutions Architect", "CKA", "CISSP"

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Don't:**
- Omit work permit status (if non-Swiss/EU)
- Use vague achievement descriptions
- Include photo that's unprofessional (selfies, vacation pics, outdated)
- Exceed 2 pages for non-executive roles
- Use inconsistent date formats
- Leave language skills unstated
- Include salary expectations in resume (cover letter only)
- Use overly creative/colorful designs (conservative market)

✅ **Do:**
- Quantify every achievement with metrics
- Tailor resume to specific job posting
- Include international experience and mobility
- Show continuous learning (courses, certifications)
- Highlight open-source contributions
- Demonstrate cultural adaptability
- Include relevant side projects for tech roles
- Add technical blog or GitHub profile if strong

## References & Testimonials

Swiss employers often request references. Common approaches:

1. **"References available upon request"**: Standard, safe option
2. **Include 2-3 references directly**: More proactive
   - Name, Title, Company, Phone, Email
   - Always ask permission first
   - Choose references who know Swiss market

## Additional Swiss-Specific Tips

### Military Service
- Swiss nationals: Include obligatory military service
- Example: "Swiss Military Service (2015-2016): IT Systems Specialist"

### Civil Status
- Previously common, now optional
- Generally omit unless specifically requested
- Conservative industries may still expect it

### Hobbies & Interests
- Optional section, can demonstrate cultural fit
- Choose professionally relevant or team-oriented activities
- Examples: "Mountain hiking, chess club president, open-source contributor"

### GitHub Footer Link
**ALWAYS include**: "Curious how this resume was built? Explore the system at github.com/datarian/CV"
- Demonstrates technical sophistication
- Shows open-source contribution mindset
- Differentiates from generic resumes
